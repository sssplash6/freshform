# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Five roles on one site. Freshman Academy staff (admin, department leaders, sales) manage students and watch hour dashboards a few times a week, mid-task, on desktop. Mentors (@freshman.academy accounts) log completed sessions and check their own totals and anonymous feedback. Students are external clients — often applying to universities abroad — who check their remaining hours, book mentors through Calendly links, and leave feedback; the app must feel approachable to them, not like an internal admin tool they were accidentally let into.

## Product Purpose
Track students' mentoring hours honestly. Admins grant per-mentor hour pools; mentors log sessions that draw them down; every change notifies the student and lands in an audit trail. Each role's dashboard answers its one question: how many hours are left, who is running low, how is my program doing. Success: the numbers are trusted by everyone, students always know where they stand, and nothing about hours happens silently.

## Brand Personality
Warm, encouraging, dependable. Mentoring is the product, so the interface should read like a supportive coach with a tidy ledger: friendly plain-language copy, generous use of the brand orange for warmth, and calm, exact numbers. Never cold, never bureaucratic — but never cute at the expense of clarity about hours.

## Anti-references
- Heavy SaaS dashboards: stat-tile grids, gradients, badge overload, chart clutter.
- LMS / school portals (Moodle, Blackboard): institutional density, tables everywhere, beige chrome.
- Startup landing-page gloss: hero sections and marketing polish leaking into an internal tool.
- Gamification: streaks, confetti, mascots, celebration animations.

## Design Principles
- Numbers are the interface: remaining and completed hours are shown plainly and prominently, never buried in decoration; overdrawn balances read red without softening.
- One accent, one meaning: orange = hours and progress, navy = actions and chrome, red = warnings/destructive. Color never decorates.
- Warmth through tone, not ornament: encouraging copy and generous whitespace carry the friendliness; containers, borders, and badges stay minimal.
- Each role's home answers its one question first; everything else is secondary navigation.
- Every hour change is visible: notifications and audit history are first-class, not an afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
No formal WCAG target. Keep AA-ish text contrast, keyboard-reachable controls, aria labels on icon-only affordances, and prefers-reduced-motion support.
